19    /**
20    * Chronology provides access to the individual date time fields for a
21    * chronological calendar system.
22    * <p>
23    * Various chronologies are supported by subclasses including ISO
24    * and GregorianJulian. To construct a Chronology you should use the
25    * factory methods on the chronology subclass in the chrono package.
26    * <p>
27    * For example, to obtain the current time in the coptic calendar system:
28    * <pre>
29    * DateTime dt = new DateTime(CopticChronology.getInstance());
30    * </pre>
31    * <p>
32    * The provided chronology implementations are:
33    * <ul>
34    * <li>ISO - The <i>de facto<i> world calendar system, based on the ISO-8601 standard
35    * <li>GJ - Historically accurate calendar with Julian followed by Gregorian
36    * <li>Gregorian - The Gregorian calendar system used for all time (proleptic)
37    * <li>Julian - The Julian calendar system used for all time (proleptic)
38    * <li>Buddhist - The Buddhist calendar system which is an offset in years from GJ
39    * <li>Coptic - The Coptic calendar system which defines 30 day months
40    * <li>Ethiopic - The Ethiopic calendar system which defines 30 day months
41    * <li>Islamic - The Islamic, or Hijri, lunar calendar system
42    * </ul>
43    * Hopefully future releases will contain more chronologies.
44    * <p>
45    * This class defines a number of fields with names from the ISO8601 standard.
46    * It does not 'strongly' define these fields however, thus implementations
47    * are free to interpret the field names as they wish.
48    * For example, a week could be defined as 10 days and a month as 40 days in a
49    * special WeirdChronology implementation. Clearly the GJ and ISO
50    * implementations provided use the field names as you would expect.
51    *
52    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.ISOChronology
53    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.GJChronology
54    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.GregorianChronology
55    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.JulianChronology
56    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.CopticChronology
57    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.BuddhistChronology
58    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.EthiopicChronology
59    * @see org.joda.time.chrono.IslamicChronology
60    *
61    * @author Stephen Colebourne
62    * @author Brian S O'Neill
63    * @since 1.0
64    */
